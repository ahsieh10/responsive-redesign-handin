Reasons for redesign

I first decided to choose this website because, as a student of this course 2 years ago who moved on to TA and HTA the course, I still have trouble navigating the page at times to find the information that I want.

After examining deeper with WAIVE and interacting with the website, these were the accessibility issues that I noticed:

  • When hovering over or clicking clickable items on the page, there was no indication of a change in the UI, so there was no way of knowing that the element was clickable.
  • The text on the sidebar is quite small.
  • The text on the page itself utilizes limited page hierarchy and is mainly perceived as a large block of text.
  • It is difficult to distinguish between the navigation bar of the website and the navigation bar of the page.
  • There is no use of a h1 header on this webpage.
  • The contrast of the text from the sidebar was not great enough.
  • The language attribute is missing.
  • There are no accessibility labels on the website.

Checkpoint 3: Development

After the hi-fi mockups, it was finally time to put the website into HTML/CSS.

I learned that the font that I had used in Figma was not available locally in CSS, so I had to import the file from Google Fonts.

Overall, it was quite difficult to put the elements that I had wanted into place. When I tried to place icons in the navigation bar, they were very distorted and I struggled to make them line up in the center of the button, so I had to forgo it.

Also, as mentioned above in checkpoint 2, I thought that I had created enough contrast in the words, but when I fed it through the accessibility detector, I was still marked for no contrast, so I had to modify the color palette a bit.

Figuring out how to move the credits bar to the bottom of the page was also quite an experience as well; I realized that I had to copy and paste the same element twice and enable/disable visibility for both to make it happen.

Also, I had to increase the height of the buttons to compensate for the text of the buttons wrapping around into the second line.

This is the final product.

Overall, I was met with many challenges, but this was quite a satisfying process.
